Toluca de Lerdo City

Historical information

Toluca, in full Toluca de Lerdo, city, capital of México estado (state), central Mexico. It is located about 30 miles (50 km) west-southwest of Mexico City, at the base of Nevado de Toluca volcano, the peak of which rises above 15,000 feet (4,570 metres) just southwest of the city. Toluca itself lies in a cool valley at an elevation of about 8,790 feet (2,680 metres), one of the highest cities in North America. Toluca was founded in 1530 and attained city status in 1533. Its oldest church was erected soon after the Spanish conquest and was rebuilt in 1585. The designation “de Lerdo” was adopted in 1861 in honour of the Mexican president Sebastián Lerdo de Tejada.
Toluca is a centre for commerce, communications, and other services, but agriculture (wheat, corn [maize], beans, and other vegetables) and livestock raising in the vicinity are also important. Industries include textile manufacturing, brewing and distilling, and food processing. It is well connected by railway and highway with Mexico City and other plateau cities. Toluca is the seat of the Autonomous University of the State of México (1828; present status 1956). Colonies of migratory monarch butterflies winter in the forested highlands of Nevado de Toluca National Park southwest of the city. Pop. (2000) 435,125; metro. area, 1,471,146; (2010) 489,333; metro. area, 1,846,116.

Demographics

Total Population

The city of Toluca had a total of 223,876 inhabitants until March 27, 2020, according to the last count and official delimitation carried out in 2020 jointly by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography, the National Population Council and the Secretariat of Social Development.
The municipality of Toluca has a total of 873,536 inhabitants according to the results of the Population and Housing Census carried out in 2015 by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography, of which 418,527 men and 455,009 women; being by both its percentage of male population of 48.3%, the annual population growth rate from 2000 to 2005 has been 20%, 28.5% of the inhabitants are under 15 years of age, while between that age and 64 years It finds 62.1% of the population, 94% of the inhabitants reside in localities with more than 2,500 inhabitants and finally 2.9% of the inhabitants older than 5 years of age are speakers of an indigenous language.

Population Distribution

It has a total of 69 rural localities, and 49 thousand 139 inhabitants reside there, representing 6.0% of the total population of the municipality, 770 thousand 422 inhabitants reside in urban locations 94.0 percent.
The towns with the largest number of population are: the Municipal Headquarters (489 333), San Pablo Autopan (35,141), San José Guadalupe Otzacatipan (31 299), San Mateo Otzacatipan (22 thousand 656), and San Pedro Totoltepec (21 thousand 76), among others, however there are scattered hamlet towns with little population as is the case of El dorado (9), Rancho la Esperanza (5) and Rancho el Rosedal (4).

Growth Rate

The municipality of Toluca, throughout its history, has had a demographic growth of great intensity, which began in 1960 due to the high rates birth rate and immigration, a result of the industrialization process in the State of Mexico, which made the municipality an important center of attraction population, from 1950 to 2010, the increase is 704 thousand 542 inhabitants and the growth rate 2000-2010 is 2.02.

Mortality

The behavior of mortality is reflected in the average number of deceased children in the female population aged 12 years and over, for the year 2000 it was 13.49 and for 2010 it is 7.9.

Migration

Toluca has a total population of 819 thousand 561 inhabitants, of which 107 thousand 235, (13.08%), were born in another entity, one thousand 3 (0.12%) in the United States of America, one thousand 353 people were born in another country (0.17%), and 16 thousand 464 inhabitants (2.01%) did not specify their place of birth.
